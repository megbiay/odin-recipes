Basque Burnt Cheesecake

Description

Originating from the Basque region of Spain, Basque Burnt Cheesecake was created in 1990 by chef Santiago Rivera of La Vi√±a restaurant.
Baked at high temperature, resulting to a burnt exterior and a soft and velvety interior. The texture of the insides of the cake can vary according to desired consistency, with adjustments to the baking time.
Can be served at rom temperature, or chilled.
Storage: refridgerated, 3 days.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb cream cheese
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 3 large eggs
  • 2 tbsp cake flour
  • 2 tsp cornstrach
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1/8 tsp salt

Procedure

  1. Preheat the oven (conventional oven) to 475F (245C).
  2. Line a 6-inch round cake pan with parchment paper.
  3. Soften room temperature cream cheese with a spatula.
  4. Add sugar, mix by spreading the cheese with the sugar. Scrape the sides of the bowl often. Ensure there are no lumps.
  5. In another bowl, whisk eggs.
  6. Gradually add the beaten eggs into the cream cheese mixture, one-third at a time. Mix thoroughly and completely upon each addition.
  7. Gradually sift in cake flour and cornstarch while stirring with a spatula.
  8. Add salt. Mix. Ensure there are no lumps.
  9. Add heavy cream while stirring. Mix until combined.
  10. Pour cake batter into prepared pan. Pour in one go to avoid creating air pockets. Tap cake pan on the countertop to eliminate air pockets. Pop air bubbles with a bamboo skewer or toothpick, run the skewer through the batter to remove any air bubbles.
  11. Bake for about 30-35 minutes, until the top is dark amber or brown, with a jiggly center.
  12. Cool to room temperature for about 2-3 hours.
