Milk Cheese Cookies

Description

A homemade version of the popular Japanese treat, Tokyo Milk Cheese Cookies.
Made with Gouda cheese, which is a more accessible variation of cheese. Cookies are made with 4 ingredients, the milk flavor coming from condensed milk. The sweetness from the condensed milk isn't too intense, which deepens the cheese flavor. Storage: refridgerated, 5 days

Ingredients

  • 70g unsalted butter
  • 110g condensed milk
  • 200g cake flour
  • 1/8 tsp salkt
  • 200g Gouda Cheese (slices)

Procedure

  1. Soften the unsalted butter with a whisk. Add condensed milk and mix. Sift in flour and salt, and fold with a spatula.
  2. Form the dough on a surface. Wrap dough in plastic cling film, roll with a rolling pin to distribute uniformly. Chill in the fridge for at least an hour.
  3. With the dough in between two parchment papers, roll to about 1/4 of an inch. Cut cookies with a cookie cutter.
  4. Bake at 170C (338F) for 13 to 15 minutes. Cool
  5. Cut the cheese with the same square cookie cutter. Assemble into sandwiches, cheese between two cookies.
